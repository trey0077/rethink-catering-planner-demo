# Validation Notes

## HoneyBaked observation

The HoneyBaked catering flow is menu/category-first ecommerce. After selecting a store, a user can browse categories like Boxed Lunches, Gluten-Friendly Menu, Breakfast, Party Trays, Buffet Salads, Deli Sides, Desserts, and Beverages. Product pages allow customization and quantity; adding 40 identical Signature Sandwich Boxed Lunches produced a cart total of $499.60.

Problem: it lets a user buy items, but does not help answer “I have 40 people coming; what should I order?” It lacks a group-order planner, variety guidance, budget/person framing, dietary coverage, or recommended packages by event type.

## Bay Gourmet observation

Bay Gourmet Catering is a local Mobile, Alabama caterer. The site has brand/photos, menu categories, a contact consultation form, and a Gourmet To-Go form. The menus page says they cater events from 40–5,000 guests and has a minimum of 40 people for all orders.

Menu categories include Weddings, Appetizers & Dips, Dinner Catering, Corporate Events, Cocktail Parties, Seafood, Frozen Take-Out, and Holiday/At-Home Parties.

Corporate Events page includes lots of menu possibilities organized by tabs such as Hors D’Oeuvres, Snacks, Dips, Seafood, Beef, Pork and Lamb, Chicken. The first tab alone lists many options like spring rolls, quiche bites, spanakopita, bruschetta, stuffed mushrooms, sushi, finger sandwiches, caprese skewers, samosas, and duck bites.

Contact form asks for event date, location, start time, expected guests, event type, and notes. Useful intake, but not decision help.

Gourmet To-Go form has 82 checkbox items and tells users to call to confirm item availability before ordering.

## Product insight

This is a data-to-decision problem:

- Caterer has menu/event/service knowledge.
- Customer has event context and uncertainty.
- Existing websites expose categories/items/forms.
- Missing layer: translate event context into a sensible catering direction and quote-ready handoff.

## Rethink positioning

Useful lines:

- “Your site shows people what you can make. It should also help them decide what to ask for.”
- “Don’t make catering buyers reverse-engineer your menu.”
- “Turn ‘I have 40 people coming’ into a consultation-ready request.”
- “Small tools for complicated buying decisions.”
- “Use your own menu and event data to give customers 80% of the decision before they call.”
